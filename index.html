Fascinating Facts About Iceland

Geography and Nature
  • Iceland is located in the North Atlantic Ocean and is one of the most volcanically active regions in the world.
  • Over 10% of Iceland is covered by glaciers, including Vatnajökull, the largest glacier in Europe.
  • The island sits on two tectonic plates (North American and Eurasian), which you can see meet at Þingvellir National Park.

Culture and History
  • Iceland was settled by Norse Vikings in the 9th century.
  • It has the world’s oldest surviving parliament, the Althingi, established in 930 AD.
  • Icelandic, the national language, has changed so little over centuries that Icelanders can still read ancient Viking texts.

Unique Lifestyle
  • Around 85% of Iceland’s energy comes from renewable sources, especially geothermal and hydropower.
  • Iceland has no standing army and is ranked among the safest countries in the world.
  • Naming laws are strict—names must be approved by the Icelandic Naming Committee to ensure they fit the language and grammar.

Wildlife and Environment
  • Iceland is home to more sheep than people, and puffins outnumber both!
  • There are no mosquitoes in Iceland.
  • The island's horses, known as Icelandic horses, are a unique and ancient breed with special gaits found nowhere else.
